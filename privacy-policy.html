6. International Data Transfers

As a South African company operating globally, Cronos IT Solutions processes personal information across multiple jurisdictions. This section provides detailed information about how we handle international data transfers.

6.1 Our Global Operations Structure

Our headquarters and primary data processing facilities are located in South Africa. However, to serve our global client base effectively, we operate through the following structure:

  • South African Headquarters: Primary operations, data center, administrative functions
  • Regional Offices: We maintain offices and operational facilities in various countries across Africa, Europe, and potentially other continents
  • Remote Teams: We employ remote workers and contractors in multiple jurisdictions
  • Cloud Infrastructure: We utilize cloud service providers with data centers in various global locations
  • Third-Party Service Providers: We engage service providers located in different countries for specialized functions

6.2 Transfers from South Africa to Other Jurisdictions

When we transfer personal information from South Africa to other countries, we ensure compliance with Section 72 of POPIA, which requires that:

  • The recipient country has adequate data protection laws substantially similar to POPIA, or
  • The recipient is subject to a binding agreement or corporate rules that provide adequate protection, or
  • You have consented to the transfer after being informed of potential risks

6.3 Adequacy Determinations and Safeguards

We transfer personal information to the following categories of jurisdictions under the following safeguards:

Jurisdiction Type Examples Transfer Mechanism
GDPR-Compliant Countries European Economic Area (EEA), UK Recognized as having adequate protection under POPIA
Countries with Adequate Laws Selected jurisdictions with comparable data protection frameworks Direct transfers based on adequacy assessment
Standard Contractual Clauses United States, Asia-Pacific regions, other African countries Data Transfer Agreements incorporating EU Standard Contractual Clauses and POPIA-compliant terms
Binding Corporate Rules Transfers to our subsidiaries and affiliates Internal data protection policies approved by regulators
Consent-Based Transfers Ad-hoc transfers to specific jurisdictions Explicit informed consent after disclosure of risks

8. Data Security Measures

We implement comprehensive technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

8.1 Technical Security Measures

  • Encryption: Data encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3 and at rest using AES-256 encryption
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication (MFA), and least privilege principles
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, network segmentation
  • Endpoint Protection: Antivirus, anti-malware, endpoint detection and response (EDR) solutions
  • Security Monitoring: 24/7 security operations center (SOC), real-time threat monitoring, SIEM systems
  • Vulnerability Management: Regular security scanning, penetration testing, patch management
  • Data Loss Prevention: DLP tools to prevent unauthorized data exfiltration
  • Backup and Recovery: Regular encrypted backups, disaster recovery procedures, business continuity plans

8.2 Organizational Security Measures

  • Security Policies: Comprehensive information security policies and procedures
  • Employee Training: Regular security awareness training and phishing simulations
  • Background Checks: Screening of employees and contractors with access to sensitive data
  • Confidentiality Agreements: All staff bound by confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements
  • Incident Response: Documented incident response plan and dedicated response team
  • Physical Security: Secured facilities with access controls, surveillance, and environmental controls
  • Third-Party Assessments: Security audits of vendors and service providers
  • Compliance Certifications: ISO 27001, SOC 2, and other relevant security certifications

8.3 Security Breach Response

In the event of a personal information breach, we will:

  • Activate our incident response team immediately
  • Conduct a thorough investigation to determine the scope and impact
  • Take immediate action to contain and remediate the breach
  • Notify the Information Regulator within 72 hours if required by POPIA
  • Notify affected individuals without undue delay if the breach poses a risk to their rights and freedoms
  • Provide information about the breach, potential consequences, and mitigation measures
  • Document the breach and our response for compliance purposes
  • Implement additional measures to prevent similar incidents

8.4 Your Security Responsibilities

While we implement robust security measures, you also have responsibilities:

  • Keep your login credentials confidential and secure
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication
  • Do not share your account access with unauthorized persons
  • Report any suspected security incidents or unauthorized access immediately
  • Keep your devices and software updated with security patches
  • Be cautious of phishing attempts and social engineering

10. Your Data Protection Rights

Under POPIA, GDPR, and other applicable data protection laws, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

10.1 Right of Access

You have the right to:

  • Request confirmation of whether we are processing your personal information
  • Obtain a copy of your personal information
  • Receive information about how we process your data
  • Request the categories of personal information, purposes of processing, and recipients of data

10.2 Right to Rectification

You have the right to:

  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information
  • Have incomplete personal information completed
  • Update your contact details and other information we hold

10.3 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal information when:

  • The information is no longer necessary for the purposes it was collected
  • You withdraw consent and there is no other legal basis for processing
  • You object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
  • The information has been unlawfully processed
  • Erasure is required to comply with a legal obligation

This right may be limited when retention is required by law or for legal claims.

10.4 Right to Restriction of Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict processing of your personal information when:

  • You contest the accuracy of the personal information
  • The processing is unlawful but you oppose erasure
  • We no longer need the data, but you need it for legal claims
  • You have objected to processing pending verification of legitimate grounds

10.5 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to:

  • Receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
  • Request transmission of your data to another controller where technically feasible

This right applies to information you have provided to us with consent or for contract performance.

10.6 Right to Object

You have the right to object to:

  • Processing based on legitimate interests
  • Direct marketing communications (including profiling)
  • Processing for scientific, historical research, or statistical purposes

We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

10.7 Right to Withdraw Consent

When processing is based on consent, you have the right to:

  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Receive confirmation of withdrawal

Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal.

10.8 Right to Complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority:

  • In South Africa: Information Regulator (South Africa)
    Email: inforeg@justice.gov.za
    Website: https://www.justice.gov.za/inforeg/
  • In the European Union: Your local data protection authority
  • In other jurisdictions: Your local privacy or data protection regulator

10.9 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights:

  • Contact our Information Officer at privacy@cronosit.co.za
  • Provide sufficient information to verify your identity
  • Specify which rights you wish to exercise
  • We will respond within one month (extendable by two months for complex requests)
  • We do not charge a fee unless your request is manifestly unfounded or excessive

10.10 Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects you. We will inform you of any automated decision-making and provide the opportunity to request human intervention.
